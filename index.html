Benefits of the AIFC

  • 01

    Lightweight exchange regine

    Transactions of AIFC participants can be denominated and executed in the currency agreed between them, including US dollars, euros, rubles and tenge.

  • 02

    Aifc law and jurisdiction

    AIFC Acts were developed based on principles of the law of England and Wales and the standards of leading global financial centers AIFC Court is the region's first independent common law judiciary for the settlement of civil and commercial disputes.

  • 03

    English as the main official language

    The official language of AIFC is English. AIFC Acts, court proceedings records, all transactions, responses to inquiries must be in English.

  • 04

    Tax benefits

    Exemption of AIFC participants from CIT payments until January 1, 2066 for financial services, ancillary services provided to licensed participants, as well as dividends capital gains on shares and securities registered on AIX of the on natural and legal persons.

  • 05

    Redomicilation

    AIFC has developed acts allowing the transfer of a company from other jurisdictions.

  • 06

    Recognized stock exchange

    UK Ministry of Revenue and Duties granted the "Astana" international exchange the status of a "recognized stock exchange".

  • 07

    Effective and digitalised regulator

    The registration process of the AIFC participants switched to an online format, and the registration period was shortened from 7 days in 2018 to 1 day in 2020.

  • 08

    Organisational-legal forms of AIFC legal entities

    AIFC has 15 types of organizational and legal forms for the creation of new legal entities. For most of the companies there is no requirements on initial capital.

  • 09

    Simplified labour and visa regime

    Exemption of AIFC participants from obtaining work permit to hire foreign employees. Working and dependent visas can be issued for a period of up to 5 years. AIFC participants enjoy simplified visa related procedures and can obtain required government service at AIFC Expat Centre on a single-window basis.

  • 10

    Financial support

    Reimbursement of part of the costs of issuers, reduction in the cost of services of financial and legal consultants.

AIFC’s delivery model

In 2020 the new strategy was adopted, marking the beginning of the establishment of AIFC as regional and global financal centre. To achieve its goals, AIFC adopts a unique approach: dual-core delivery model, which focuses on reaching two key directions:

  • Increasing connectivity by developing as an Investment Hub

  • Increasing traffic and business activity by developing as a regional financial centre

A metaphor can explain AIFC’s plan to achieve each of these two models: AIFC providing connectivity like an airport and AIFC serving the customers like a shopping mall.

What is the difference between airport and shopping center models? The main stakeholders of the airport are airlines, which serve customers and connect cities and countries. In other words, Connectivity is enabled via airlines.

The success of airports lies in providing the best conditions, so that both customers and airlines will prefer transit routes through such hub. AIFC aims to become convenient and modern hub when it comes to attracting investments. High quality service will lay upon basic principles: market positioning, diversity and differentiation.

On the other side, shopping centre model focuses on ensuring business activity and maintaining sufficient level of customers’ traffic. The model is centered to on providing services to end customers local or worldwide ones. Worth to note, range of products and services available to customers, physical and digital infrastructure are the main enablers to success.

Further development of the AIFC to the level of an international investment hub and a regional financial center should take into account technological progress and stimulate international recognition and trust.

Our Partners

Brokerage

Favourable ecosystem with no tax on capital gains/dividend payments and multi-currency trading operations allows regional and international brokerage firms to gain a competitive cost of operation and gives access to Central Asia market.

Asset Management

Opportunities for global as well as local private equity, hedge fund and real estate fund managers to add diversity to their portfolios with an extensive range of products and services (investment & portfolio advisory, tax & wealth planning, asset allocation).

Capital Markets

Astana International Exchange (hereinafter — AIX), operating within AIFC, offers investors and issuers all the main instruments of capital market: Shares, GDRs, Exchange-Traded Funds (ETFs), debt securities (including corporate bonds, green bonds, Sukuk, Exchange-Traded Notes (ETNs), sovereign bonds and Eurobonds).

Private Banking

The development of the Private banking sector and an extensive range of products and services (investment & portfolio advisory, tax & wealth planning, asset allocation) are offered via models and structures such as representatives, advisory, branches and development of booking and custody for the local players in line with global best practices of a modern wealth management.

Banking

New entities, as well as existing bank representatives, from all around the globe to take advantage of the positive economic change in Central Asia and fulfill the increasing demand for a range of commercial and wholesale banking services.

Islamic Finance

With expanding of Islamic finance industry in the region, the AIFC has developed stable connections with a range of participants, including Islamic banks, asset management companies, investment funds, takaful and retakaful companies and other Islamic financial organizations, as well as leading international Islamic financial institutions.

Insurance, Reinsurance

The AIFC allows foreign institutions to use its financial services and take advantage of the privileges offered within its territory. AIFC participants are offered with insurance coverage on different aspects such as: monetary, legal, transport, physical and materialistic.

Financial Technology

The AIFC is the first jurisdiction in the Eurasian region to support regulatory regimes for new forms of investments, including crowdfunding, Initial Coin Offerings, trading with digital assets, testing and developing FinTech services under the regulatory sandbox regime called FinTech Lab, and cross-border testings of FinTech activities in several jurisdictions under the Global Financial Innovation Network (a network of financial regulators).

Green Finance

In light of Central Asia region transition towards green economy and developing need for financing of “green” projects, AIFC Green Finance Centre provides initial assistance to potential issuers, investors and market players on preparations for the issuance of green bonds on AIX. The Centre also covers expenses incurred by issuers in providing the mandatory external review of green bonds.

Ancillary Service Providers

AIFC welcomes AIFC participants who provide ancillary services such as legal, audit, accountancy, consulting credit rating.

Holding Companies, Corporate Offices and other commercial non-regulated activities

AIFC offers much space for entities to situate their corporate offices and regional headquarters. The heart of Central Asia offers an ideal location to generate successful business ventures, realize investment opportunities, as well as provide the most secure licensing and regulatory environment. The Centre provides a full range of services for its partners, in order for them to enjoy the facilities necessary to efficiently conduct their businesses.
